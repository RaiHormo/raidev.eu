About Mira's Journal

View on github

This is a game I've been working on as a side-project for a while. It's a turn-based RPG, somewhat inspired by ATLUS games.
It features calendar progression and a color-based battle system.

Early Screenshots



Story

A young wolf girl named Mira Levenor who finds herself in a strange realm in ruins, with a few people stuck there trying to survive.
As each day passes the surroundings get more and more corrupted.

Follow her day to day life exploring the lost realm, fighting in turn-based battles and getting to know new people as they try to find a way to the outside world and discover the truth.

During her adventures, Mira will keep a journal, giving her thoughts on everything that happens every day.

Current state

The game is still in early development, and all the work so far has been done by one person.

My history with game development

I first started making games as a kid with scratch. I never made anything major though, my most notable projects were a Mario fangame I only made 3 levels for and a simple PVP game.

The only other game I've made was a Pokemon fangame called "Pokemon Redder and Bluer", stupid name I know.
It was my interpretation as a 14 year old of what a Kanto game would look like 15 years after the events of the original.

I never finished it due to lack of motivation after a while, and it's now basically lost media to anyone but me since the Relic Castle website shut down.

Another game I have attempted to make was something I called "Project BZ". It mostly stayed as a concept, but it was a sci-fi RPG with a focus on action commands. I designed a lot of characters and locations for it, but having no real gamedev experience, the scope was way too high.

The initial concept for Mira's Journal

With Project BZ being such a dream project, I wanted to make a more down to earth RPG.
Of course any kind of RPG is still a huge project, and not what most people would recommend as a beginner game.

It was October of 2021 when I drew the first sketches of some of the character for Mira's Journal.

The first characters I made for this game were Mira, Daze and Alcine.



Mira was a young wolf girl with some fire powers who ends up getting mixed up in Daze's affairs.



Daze was meant to be a stereotypical hero, the usual leader of the party with a special destiny.



Alcine has changed a lot through development. What's stayed the same is that they're a spirit creature with a liquid body.
But originally they had a more mature look with something that resembles long hair. But I later decided to make them more like a child originally that grows along the journey.

The RPG Maker MZ version of the game

In early 2022 I bought the RPG Maker MZ engine with the intention of using it to make this game.

The reason I picked it was simple:
I wanted to make a top down rpg that I thought was not too complex, and this seemed to be what this engine was designed for.

I worked on this version of the game for about 9 months, with some results.
However RPG Maker turned out to be quite limiting for what I wanted to do.

Working on a game on it feels more like modding an existing game rather than creating something new, as the pre-existing systems are designed for a very specific type of battle system. Creating mechanics and especially UI were pretty tedious, and working with plug-ins such as VizuStella was just annoying, so I ended up scrapping that version of the game.

The Godot version

After I was done with my entrance exams in the 15th of July 2023, I instantly jumped into this engine I had never used before called Godot.

Why Godot? There are Plenty of reasons.

One was the fact that it was open source, which comes with the usual benefits like being completly free and independent from a company's dumb decisions.
Open source software also often works great on Linux, which I had started using as my main operating system around that time. Godot had also just released the 4.0 update back then, so it felt like a great chance to jump in.

I had absolutely no programming knowlegde at the time, but I felt like it was a perfect chance to learn.

So I just started working on this game again and I slowly learned how to use most things in the engine. This time I had to implement stuff like saving and inventories myself, which I had taken for granted in RPG Maker, but it's honestly more fun this way.

And that brings us to the present, thanks for reading!

Mira Levenor


Age: 19
Species: Cyrenian
Pronouns: She/Her
Aura affinity: Heat
Birthday: October 18

Mira is the most knowledgable of the party as well as the main damage dealer. However she struggles quite a bit in social situations and can let her emotions control her easily.
Trying to improve herself, she started keeping a personal journal, not knowing where that would lead her...

Being from a species whoose homeland is now gone, she lived in a small village along with other remaining Cyrenians.
She worked as a librarian in a nearby city.
She has an interest in science, magic and monsters, however she doesn't get many opportunities to use her theoretical knowledge.

Alcine


Age: less than 1
Species: Spirit
Pronouns: They/Them
Aura affinity: Freeze / Spiritual

A strange spirit creature Mira found when first entering the Lost Realm. The player as Mira will have the option to name this character, but the canon name is Alcine.

Their body is made entirely out of water, that they control the shape and temperature of.
While not able to speak, Alcine behaves a lot like a young child and clings to Mira a lot since the day they met.

Alcine's origins are unknown, even to themself. But sometimes they have visions of another life, not always pleasant ones.

Asteria Versea


Age: 17~
Species: Frayse
Pronouns: She/Her
Aura affinity: Physical
Birthday: Unknown

Asteria has been almost her entire life in the Lost Realm, seperated from the rest of the world. She adopted as a baby by a human named Feni Versea and raised around Pyrson. Being so isolated as a child, she didn't have many friends. One day, in the storage building in Pyrson, she saw a little ghost that sneaked in who introduced themself as Yaru.
Asteria and Yaru became friends, with Asteria growing more attached to the spirits in the Realm, leading her to become a summoner.

She can often put of a tough exterior but she often lacks confidence in her decisions, relying on the people around her a bit too much. She is also quite clueless about things, having grown up so isolated.

Daze (David Zepher)


Age: 18
Species: Human
Pronouns: He/Him
Aura affinity: Electric
Birthday: January 4

Daze was told from a young age that he would need to be a hero.
The one who would free Iseferia from the corrupt Monarchy. Not only did that put a lot of pressure on him as a child but as the word got out that he is the chosen hero, he and his family were hunted to no end. Eventually he found a group of people that he could rely on and he aims to complete his set goal.
However after an incident he was seprated from his party and ended up with Mira in the Lost Realm, where he saved her from certain death.

Daze is calm and collected, in a way that might seem cold to some, but he is a caring person in reality. He speaks in a pretty formal way out of habit. Sometimes he might try to be more casual but it only end up making situations more awkward.

How is it going?

As fast as a large scale game being worked on my a single person on their free time realistically can.

Eventually I want to have devlogs in this page, but for now you can join my Discord server where I talk about my work on the game as it happens.

When will this be released?

I honestly have no idea. Game development is very complicated and I'm currently doing this myself entirely as a hobby. It'll probably take years.
Maybe some sort of demo release is closer, but don't hold your breath.

What platforms will it be available on?

Well the main target during development is Linux and Windows, with it releasing on Steam.
Mac is probably possible but I would have nothing to test it on. An android release is also something I would want to do after launch, but it would need a seprate UI mode for it to work.

Consoles? I wish I had the budget for that.

How will it release?

I'm still thinking about that, but I think the release will be split into the 3 in-game months: November, December and January.
These are the 3 major acts in the story, and I think focusing on one at a time will be less overwhelming.

What is the game being made with?

I'm using Godot as the game engine (The 4.x series and I keep it up-to-date).
For art I use Aseprite for pixelart and Krita for everything else.
Beyond that I use Obsidian for documentation and Audacity for audio editing, although I'm no audio expert.

Will more people join the team?

I do want to work with more people eventually.
I would definitely need someone to make music as I'm completly clueless in that area.
However I want to have a playable demo first so be sure the concept of the game works as I see it.
